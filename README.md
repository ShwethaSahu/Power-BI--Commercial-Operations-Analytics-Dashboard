End-to-End Vendor Performance & Commercial Intelligence Project

This project covers the entire data pipeline , starting from raw database extraction and data cleaning to exploratory analysis, statistical testing, and final interactive dashboarding. 

The aim of this analysis is to evaluate commercial metrics, identify low-performing vendors, mitigate supply chain risks, and provide data-backed recommendations to maximize gross margins.
This project is divided into distinct analytical phases based on professional data engineering and science workflows:

1. SQL Data Analysis & Cleaning :-
* Built and structured the relational database to handle complex vendor and transactional tables.
* Cleaned raw datasets by handling missing values, filtering anomalies, and optimizing data types for faster querying.
* Wrote complex joins and aggregate queries to stage data for deeper analysis.

2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) with Python:-
* Utilized Pandas to manipulate large data subsets and extract fundamental statistics.
* Designed custom visualizations using Matplotlib and Seaborn to spot underlying data distributions, sales patterns, and outliers in pricing vs. sales.

3. Hypothesis Testing & Confidence Intervals:-
* Moved beyond descriptive statistics by applying rigorous statistical methods.
* Conducted Hypothesis Testing to mathematically prove vendor efficiencies and determine if variations in profit margins across brands were statistically significant.
* Established Confidence Intervals to evaluate inventory risks and forecast purchase price margins with measurable certainty.

4. Power BI Executive Dashboard:-
* Developed an interactive dashboard reporting key commercial metrics: **Total Sales ($13.51M), Total Purchases ($9.23M), Gross Profit ($4.28M), and an overall ,Profit Margin of 53.05%.
* Formulated clean DAX measures to compute complex KPIs like Stock Turnover and MoM margin trends.
* Segmented vendors dynamically to clearly spotlight low-performing suppliers where capital is tied up in slow-moving inventor


 Core Insights:-
Profitability Peak: Commercial profit margins steadily scaled throughout the first half of the year, hitting an optimal peak of 16.70% in May.
Inventory Risk Allocation: Pinpointed high-cost, low-turnover vendors holding significant amounts of unsold inventory, identifying critical areas for contract renegotiation.
Pricing Efficiency:The relationship between purchase price and ultimate sales dollars confirmed a highly predictable return on specific top-tier vendors, while proving others fail to scale efficiently.

  Recommendations
1. Optimize Low-Turnover Accounts: Address vendors highlighted in the risk grid to re-negotiate batch sizes or establish flexible return parameters on slow-moving products.
2. Standardize Peak Purchasing Models: Analyze the inventory mix and supply strategies utilized during May to replicate the peak 16.70% profit margin across less performant quarters.
